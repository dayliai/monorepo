### Example: Caregiver Tips

**Subject:** 4 bathing safety tips from caregivers who've been there  
**Preview text:** A non-slip hack, a temperature trick, and two things most people forget.

1. **The towel bar test** — If you're relying on a towel bar for support in the shower, it's not designed for that. A suction-cup grab bar takes 30 seconds to install and could prevent a fall.  
   *Shared by: Diana, caregiver for her mom*

2. **Temperature first, always** — Run the water and test it before anyone gets in. Reduced sensation is more common than people realize, especially post-stroke.  
   *Shared by: Mark, OT and DLL advisor*

3. **The shower chair rotation** — If the person you're caring for resists a shower chair, try a rotating bath seat. It feels less "clinical" and the swivel makes transfers easier.  
   *Shared by: James, caring for his wife*

4. **Non-slip beyond the mat** — Put non-slip strips inside the tub AND on the floor right outside it. Most falls happen during the step out, not inside.  
   *Shared by: Community poll, 200+ responses*

## Stage Advancement Triggers

A Nurture subscriber advances to Cultivate when they:
- Open 3+ Nurture emails AND click at least 1 solution link
- Click any "Share a Solution" or "Share a Tip" CTA
- Reply to any email (manual tag by team)
- Have been in Nurture for 6+ weeks with consistent opens
